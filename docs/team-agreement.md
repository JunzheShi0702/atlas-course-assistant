## Team Values

- Raising questions and having different opinions is encouraged. There are no stupid questions.
- Thoughtful debate is encouraged when exploring various project ideas and implementations, but members should not attack one another outside the scope of the project, in terms of personality, culture, or values.
- All team members should actively participate in reviewing teammates’ work when requested, and feedback should focus on code quality and project goals rather than personal criticism.
- Regardless of role, each member shares responsibility for the overall quality of the team’s deliverables. Team members should be open to different ideas and approaches and recognize that compromise is often necessary for team success. When sharing ideas, members will remain respectful.
- Respect others’ time. Let others know beforehand of any scheduling disagreements. We understand that schedule conflicts or emergencies may happen, and we will work collaboratively to resolve any issues.
- If any team members are facing any physical or mental difficulties, let other members know as soon as possible. Team members should work together to address the issues (e.g., redistributing work, discussing other plans) and maintain necessary confidentiality.
- We aim to create an inclusive and welcoming environment for all team members to contribute, learn, and improve as modern software engineers.

## Team Communication

### Meeting Schedule

- The team members will meet on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and potentially Fridays each week to review current progress, make necessary changes and alignments, and plan for further deployments. If more meetings are necessary, an additional day will be identified based on members’ availability.
  - Part of the Tuesdays and Thursdays meetings are dedicated to communication with our beloved instructor, Dr. Madooei, or a CA about the project status.
- If a task is blocked for more than 2-3 hours, the team member should proactively post an update in Slack describing what they tried, what is failing, and what help is needed.
- All team members are expected to attend meetings on time, come prepared, and complete their assigned tasks as agreed upon. Failure to do so may impact team progress, especially when future work depends on completed tasks. During the meeting, all team members are anticipated to actively participate, raise questions, and contribute ideas.
  - Team members will be responsible for creating brief meeting notes (md file) that include progress, existing issues, development plans for the next meeting, and any other necessary components.
  - Team members will take notes under the following cycle:  
    Rachael → Junzhe → James → Jennifer → Alina.
  - Absences from the meetings should be communicated at least 12 hours in advance, unless an emergency arises.
  - After an absence, the member should review the meeting notes and provide comments on the meeting documentation.

### Primary Communication Tools

- According to course policies, Slack will be the primary communication tool.
  - Phone calls or text messages should only be used in urgent situations.
  - If the message is particularly targeting someone, use @ to remind that member. The member is expected to respond within the next 24 hours.
  - Use 👍 to indicate agreement, use 🤔️ to indicate confusion, and use 👀 to indicate that you will look into the issue.
  - Use ✅ to indicate that something is completed, and use ⏩️ to indicate that something is urgent.
- All issues/tasks assigned to the individual should be addressed properly before each meeting. If there are any issues, concerns, or difficulties, prior notice should be given at least 12 hours in advance.

## Coding Practices

- Every team member should take ownership of every line of code they write and every git push operation, including:  
  1\) Well-documented commits with understandable brief summaries (“Modify the frontend login panel styling” rather than “improve visual effect”).  
  2\) Meaningful comments for lines of code added, modified, or deleted as appropriate. For instance, summarizing an operation’s purpose or what it improves/clarifies/optimizes.
- AI-generated outputs should be treated as suggestions and must be verified by the developer before integration. Sensitive information such as API keys, passwords, or private data should never be entered into generative AI tools.
- Prettier will be used to unify source code styling.
