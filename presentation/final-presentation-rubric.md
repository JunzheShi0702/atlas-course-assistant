# Product Final Presentation Rubric and Criteria

Source: `product-final-presentation.pdf`

## Format and Participation Criteria

- Presentation length: **35-45 minutes**
- Q&A: **15 minutes** (can be integrated during talk)
- All team members must participate with balanced speaking time
- Live demo must use deployed app (local backup recommended in case of outage)
- Slides/visuals are required for technical section

## Required Structure

## 1) Opening Remarks (2-3 minutes)

Cover:

- Project refresher
- Problem and target users
- Why AI is essential (not bolted on)
- One speaker leads opening

Potential deductions:

- `-5` Opening lacked organization/preparation
- `-5` Motivation not communicated clearly/briefly
- `-3` Failed to re-establish why AI is essential

## 2) What the Application Does (10-20 minutes)

Cover:

- Brief recap, then demo **4-5 functional requirements**
- At least **3** demoed requirements should exercise meaningful AI features
- Focus on high-value features, not trivial flows (unless central)
- Each member should demo one requirement (~3-4 minutes each)

Potential deductions:

- `-20` No live demo
- `-7` Demoed local version instead of deployed app (unless issue was beyond team control)
- `-7` App crashed/failed more than once during demo
- `-5` Demo focused on trivial features (e.g., signup/login)
- `-7` Fewer than four requirements demoed (unless justified by scope/time complexity)
- `-7` Fewer than three requirements used meaningful AI
- `-5` Failed to follow demo instructions promptly
- `-5` Could not answer clarifying demo questions clearly

## 3) How the Application Works (15-25 minutes)

Slides are expected here. Cover:

- Software architecture and tech stack
- Focused architecture diagram(s), not cluttered mega-diagrams
- AI architecture:
  - models/providers and rationale
  - prompting strategy and structured outputs
  - memory/state handling
  - retrieval pipeline (if applicable)
  - tool-calling/agent workflow, loop controls, failure handling, guardrails
  - AI evaluation/testing approach
  - observability (cost/latency/failure monitoring)
  - safety/reliability controls
- Engineering practices:
  - non-AI testing approach and coverage screenshot
  - noteworthy APIs/algorithms/data structures
  - design trade-offs (cost/quality, latency/accuracy, autonomy/determinism, etc.)

Potential deductions:

- `-10` No slides or visuals for technical section
- `-5` Software architecture/stack not clearly stated
- `-5` High-level system design not described succinctly
- `-5` Missing or inconsistent diagrams
- `-12` AI architecture inadequately explained
- `-7` Did not explain AI testing/evaluation/monitoring
- `-7` Could not justify design choices/trade-offs in questions
- `-7` Missing relevant implementation details (libraries/APIs/algorithms/data structures)
- `-7` Wrong detail level or poor technical delivery flow

## 4) Closing Remarks (3-5 minutes)

Cover:

- Limitations and unfinished edges
- Future work / next steps after course
- Reflection and key team takeaways

Potential deductions:

- `-12` Project status/limitations not communicated succinctly
- `-5` Future work not described
- `-7` Reflection missing or shallow
- `-5` Closing lacked organization/preparation

## Cross-Cutting Delivery Deductions

- `-5` Presentation failed to engage audience
- `-7` Poor coherence/transitions between speakers
- `-7` Audience had repeated difficulty following demo
- `-5` Dominated by one or two members (unless team has fewer than five members)
- `-5` Exceeded allotted time

## Warning

- Any absent member without prior notice receives an individual zero for this activity.
